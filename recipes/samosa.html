Samosa Recipe

Samosa is a crispy, deep-fried snack with a flaky pastry shell filled with a savory mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and various spices. It is a popular street food in India and has variations found in many regions around the world. The dish is often served with chutneys and is a beloved tea-time treat.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Oil
  • Ghee
  • Water
  • Potatoes
  • Green Peas
  • Coriender Powder
  • Cumin Powder
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Garam Masala
  • Chilli Powder
  • Onion
  • Ginger
  • Garlic

Steps to make samosa:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, ajwain seeds, oil (or ghee), and salt. Mix thoroughly for 1–2 minutes.
  2. Gradually add water, starting with ¼ cup, and mix. Add another ¼ cup of water and knead until the dough is tight. If needed, add 1–2 tbsp of water to adjust consistency.
  3. Knead the dough well and let it rest for 15–20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll out each ball into a thin sheet. Cut the sheet into halves to make samosa sheets.
  5. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds, green chilies, and ginger. Stir fry for a minute.
  6. Add the mashed potatoes, green peas, and spices. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes. Add coriander leaves and mint leaves, then turn off the heat.
  7. Let the filling cool down.
  8. Take a samosa sheet and fold it into a cone shape. Brush the edges with water and seal them tightly.
  9. Fill the cone with the potato mixture, ensuring not to overfill. Seal the edges with water.
  10. Heat oil in a pan over medium-low heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the samosas in the pan and fry until golden brown.
  11. Remove the samosas with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  12. Serve hot with chutney or your favorite dipping sauce.
